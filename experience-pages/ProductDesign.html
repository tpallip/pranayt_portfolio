Product Design Intern Trestle Labs, 2020

Kibo Reader For Visually Impaired

New Delhi, India

During my internship at Trestle Labs from February to July 2020, I developed a portable phone stand specifically designed for visually impaired users , enabling them to scan books and documents independently while on the move.

The idea was to create a device that allowed users to position their phones correctly for scanning, whether they were traveling or in a non-lab setup where access to the Kibo XS reader device might be limited.

In this role, I conducted a detailed study of ergonomic design principles , focusing on creating an intuitive, user-friendly product that catered to the unique needs of visually impaired individuals.

I paid close attention to features like stability, ease of use, and portability , which were crucial for effective scanning in various environments. Additionally, I explored intuitive device operation , aiming to make the setup straightforward and functional without requiring visual guidance.
