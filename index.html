About Me

Hey There,

I'm Pranay Tummalapalli, an embedded software and control systems engineer with over 4 years of experience in robotics. I’m deeply passionate about robotic control systems, algorithmic control, and building reliable, real-world robotic systems. From humanoid upper body limbs all the way to robotic assisted surgery for laparoscopic and orthopaedic surgery, and autonomous vehicles.

Social Work

Contribution Towards Society

Throughout my journey as a socially conscious engineer, I have worked to combine technical innovation with a commitment to meaningful social impact.

My projects focus on solving real challenges faced by underrepresented communities. I developed assistive technologies for the visually impaired to enhance independence and improve daily interactions. In addition, I built a robotic hand capable of translating spoken language into sign language in real time, helping bridge communication gaps for the hearing-impaired community.

Through my work with various social organisations and my drive to create human-centred technology, I aim to build inclusive, accessible solutions that empower individuals and foster greater equity in society.

Inclusive education for the hearing-disabled

In January 2021, I co-founded Akshar Bionics with five teammates to bridge learning gaps for children with hearing disabilities. Together, we developed a robotic hand that converts spoken language into sign language in real time, enabling more inclusive and accessible classroom experiences.

Our journey with this idea began in 2018, when we won 1st place at the Smart India Hackathon – Hardware Edition held at IIT Kanpur. With a grant of INR 10 Lakhs from the Ministry Innovation Council, we advanced our prototype using rapid 3D printing techniques and designed a modular robotic arm with potential applications in prosthetics. This project played a pivotal role in strengthening my skills in mechanical design, embedded software, and robotics, and greatly shaped my engineering mindset toward developing practical, user-centric solutions.

Wearable device to assist visually impaired with object interaction

Project Raah was developed to support visually impaired individuals with object localization, recognition, and interaction through an innovative wrist-wearable device equipped with a camera. The goal was to enable users to identify and interact with objects around them more independently and confidently.

Our team worked closely with the Blind Relief Association and the Blind Welfare Society in Delhi, spending time with visually impaired individuals to understand their daily challenges and refine the design accordingly. This collaboration was instrumental in building a solution that was not only technically sound but also practical, intuitive, and genuinely impactful for the community it aimed to serve.
